§ 01 — The problem

The capstone lives or dies on trainer quality.

Traditional CCO capstone exercises are delivered as a binder of injects and a scenario briefing. The trainer reads from the binder, releases injects manually, and scores students from memory. Some squadrons have a deployed CCO who was at PSAB and knows the material cold. Others have whoever was available that week.

The gap between the best delivery and the worst is enormous. That gap is the problem. The solution is not better trainers — it is a tool that makes the weakest trainer effective and the strongest trainer exceptional.

§ 02 — The system

Three products. One engine. One content backbone.

CCO Capstone is not one tool. It is a system of three connected products — one for the trainer, one for the student workstation, and one for the student's phone — all driven by a single exercise engine and a shared library of structured content.

01 · Trainer Dashboard

The cockpit.

Master clock, inject timeline, phone scripts, teaching points, wrong-answer walkthroughs, and white cell controls. The trainer observes and adjudicates. The exercise drives the tempo.

Browser · Desktop →
02 · Student Workstation

The inbox that never stops.

A desktop-style home screen with an Outlook-like mail panel, a notification tray, and a pushed-documents area. Students see incoming stimuli in one place and do their real work in the tools they will actually have downrange.

Browser · Desktop →
03 · Mobile PWA Hero feature

A phone that buzzes in your pocket.

A progressive web app installed to the student's home screen. Push notifications when the Wing CC texts at 0300. SMS-style threads with each key player. This is the moment the training stops feeling like a classroom and starts feeling like a deployment.

PWA · Phone →
§ 03 — How it works

One engine. One content library. Three synchronized views.

When the trainer fires an inject, it lands in the right place on every device simultaneously. An email shows up on the student workstation. A text buzzes on the phone. The trainer sees the pace meter tick. No network, no login — just three browser windows on the trainer's laptop sharing state through the browser itself.

§ 04 — What's different

Built by someone who has done the job.

Ethics are buried, not bolted on

No ethics module. Ethics traps live inside regular injects.

The vendor who is related to the base liaison. The expired BPA someone wants you to use anyway. Students have to recognize them without being told.

The weakest trainer is supported

Every inject ships with the trainer's answer key.

Expected actions, teaching points, wrong-answer walkthroughs, curveball variants, and "what really happened" cards. The trainer observes. The engine delivers.

The exercise drives the tempo

You either keep up or you fall behind.

The clock runs. Injects fire on schedule. Falling behind is itself a teaching moment because it mirrors real deployed contracting. There is no pause button for reality.

Loadout-aware scenarios

No body armor? The exercise adapts.

Trainers set the squadron's deployment posture at STARTEX. Gaps generate additional injects about workarounds. Same engine, different training for every squadron.

FAR 1.102(d) is the throughline

Train executioners, not bureaucrats.

Every scoring rubric rewards defensible decision-making and appropriate pushback. Students who hide behind process are flagged for hotwash. Students who make the call are the standard.

Real war stories

"What really happened" cards from actual deployments.

Every inject is based on or inspired by a real deployed contracting scenario. At the end of each inject, the trainer reads the real-world outcome. This is the moment the training lands.

§ 05 — Customize your exercise

Make it yours. No coding required.

Every squadron's deployment is different. These tools let you tailor the exercise to your unit's mission, equipment, and training objectives — without touching a single line of code. Open a tool, make your changes, save, and you're done.

Shared Drive (K: Drive)

Manage the files students see during the exercise — contract folders, BPAs, purchase orders, templates, and reference documents. Drag and drop files from your desktop. Add folders. Rename. Delete. Everything your students need, organized the way you want.

Open K Drive Manager →

Injects & Scenario

Edit the exercise timeline — add injects, change triggers, rewrite emails, adjust phone scripts, and set teaching points. Build your own scenario from scratch or tweak the default Eagle Crest package to match your training objectives.

Open Exercise Builder →

Roster & STARTEX Config

Set up your student roster, assign shops, configure the exercise clock, and select which inject packages to include. This is the first thing you do before any exercise — it shapes everything downstream.

Open STARTEX Config →
